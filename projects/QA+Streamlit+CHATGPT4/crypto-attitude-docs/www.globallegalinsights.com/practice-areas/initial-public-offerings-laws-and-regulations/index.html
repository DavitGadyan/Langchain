Preface

We are pleased to present the eighth edition of Global Legal Insights Initial Public Offerings.

An initial public offering is a key way for companies to raise capital in the global capital markets and list their shares for public trading. Although IPOs are conceptually similar whether made to investors in New York, London or Hong Kong, or in other long-established or newer markets around the world, the regulatory frameworks, market practices, investor communities and subsequent public-company obligations are far from homogenous across jurisdictions.

This Initial Public Offerings book provides general counsels, investment bankers, lawyers, business professionals, the investing community and other advisers and interested parties with an overview of the key steps, legal issues and market practices involved in the IPO process by examining practices in 16 jurisdictions around the world, with one Expert Analysis chapter focusing on the United States and a Foreword from SIFMA.

Leading practitioners from each jurisdiction provide their expertise and guidance on navigating their local market practices and regulatory framework. Each chapter follows a similar structure: introduction of the IPO market in the relevant jurisdiction; description of the IPO process and key parties; discussion of the relevant regulators and key regulations; public company responsibilities; and potential risks, liabilities and pitfalls.

We hope you find that this book will equip you with an understanding of the legal and market fundamentals necessary for a successful IPO.

Ilir Mujalovic A&O Shearman
Harald Halbhuber A&O Shearman
